TYPOLOGY: BUST-OUT FRAUD (CREDIT CARD)
Source: FinCEN Advisory FIN-2019-A001 | Federal Reserve Supervision Manual | FICO Fraud Detection Guidelines
Category: Credit Card Fraud — Deliberate Default / Credit Exploitation

DEFINITION:
Bust-out fraud is a form of credit fraud in which an individual (or organized ring) deliberately builds a positive credit history over weeks or months — making small purchases and on-time payments — to earn trust, receive credit limit increases, and then rapidly max out the account across high-value or liquid purchases before disappearing and defaulting intentionally. The "bust-out" phase typically occurs in a compressed window of 3-14 days.

For Private Label Credit Card (PLCC) issuers like those in the retail card space, bust-out fraud disproportionately impacts cards with high credit limits and flexible spending at partner retailers and off-network merchants.

THE BUST-OUT LIFECYCLE:
Phase 1 — Account Opening (Weeks 1-4):
  - Account opened with plausible profile; may use synthetic identity
  - Initial credit limit is modest ($2,000–$5,000)
  - Thin file or recently established credit history

Phase 2 — Credit Building (Months 1-3):
  - Regular small purchases at the partner retailer (on-brand behavior)
  - On-time minimum payments or slightly above minimum
  - Requests for Credit Limit Increases (CLI) granted based on good behavior

Phase 3 — Bust-Out (Days 1-14):
  - Rapid spending across high-risk MCCs: gift cards, electronics, cash advances
  - Shift to off-network merchants (inconsistent with PLCC usage pattern)
  - Spending velocity 5-10x above established baseline
  - Cash advances (often the first-ever on account)
  - Purchases of liquid/resalable assets (jewelry, electronics, gift cards)
  - Account hits 90-100% utilization within days

Phase 4 — Disappearance:
  - Phone number disconnected or not answering
  - Email address bouncing
  - Address may be vacant or non-existent
  - No minimum payment received at due date
  - Account goes delinquent within 30-60 days of bust-out

KEY RED FLAGS FOR PLCC ISSUERS:
- Utilization spike from <30% to >90% within 7-14 days
- Gift card purchases (MCC 5999 / open-loop prepaid): highest risk indicator
- First-ever cash advance coinciding with spending spike
- Off-network spending surge (credit card used at non-partner retailers)
- Payment-to-charge ratio drops below 10% in month of bust-out
- Multiple CLI events in <90 days (rapid limit building)
- Contact channels become non-responsive post-bust-out
- SSN issuance date anomaly or thin file (synthetic identity marker)
- Account tenure of 60-180 days is the highest-risk window for bust-out

ORGANIZED RING INDICATORS:
- Multiple accounts with similar opening dates at same institution
- Shared device fingerprint or IP address during application
- Similar SSN patterns (sequential or clustered in same SSN range)
- Identical spending trajectories across "different" customers
- Referral patterns: ring members referring each other for account openings

MCC RISK HIERARCHY FOR BUST-OUT (HIGHEST TO LOWEST):
1. Open-loop gift cards / prepaid (MCC 5999 variants) — immediately liquidatable
2. Cash advances (MCC 6010, 6011) — direct cash extraction
3. Jewelry and precious metals (MCC 5094) — high resale value
4. Consumer electronics (MCC 5734) — high demand resale
5. Cryptocurrency exchanges (MCC 6051) — instant conversion

SAR FILING GUIDANCE:
File SAR when: Bust-out score ≥50 AND utilization >90% AND at least one contact channel non-responsive. Include total charge-off exposure in SAR. Reference FinCEN Advisory FIN-2019-A001 in SAR narrative. Deadline: 30 days from first suspicion.

LOSS MITIGATION:
Upon SAR filing, initiate: (1) account freeze, (2) credit bureau fraud alert, (3) internal collections referral, (4) shared intelligence with ACFE Financial Crimes network. Early detection within the bust-out window (before disappearance) can recover 40-70% of exposure.

CASE TYPOLOGY EXAMPLE:
A Synchrony PLCC cardholder (account tenure: 4 months) received two credit limit increases (CLIs) within 60 days of account opening, raising the limit from $3,000 to $8,000. During months 1-3, the account showed normal purchase behavior at the partner retailer with consistent on-time payments. In a 10-day window, the cardholder made 7 purchases totaling $7,892 — including 3 gift card transactions ($3,162), 1 cash advance ($1,500), and 4 off-network retail purchases ($3,230). Utilization reached 98.7%. The cardholder's phone is now disconnected and email is bouncing. SSN issuance date anomaly was noted at onboarding (SSN issued 2019 for individual born 1989) — consistent with synthetic identity use.
